1. Introduction

The NIT Chess Battle is a chess competition organized to promote chess at Numidia Institute of Technology (NIT) and introduce students to the benefits and history of this strategic game. The goal is to encourage chess practice while fostering critical thinking and a competitive spirit.

2. Key Information

  • Location: Cyber Park
  • Date: February 27
  • Main Objective: Encourage as many students as possible to engage in an intellectual sport that enhances their strategic and cognitive abilities.
  • Target Audience: All NIT students.

3. Event Structure

During the training session, participants will have several options:

  • Attend the training to learn chess fundamentals and strategies.
  • Play chess freely in dedicated rooms with chess tables.
  • Enjoy video games or the card game Uno in separate rooms.

During the competition:

  • A referee will be present at each chess table.
  • All participants will be added to a well-organized Discord server to track match progress, receive notifications for their turn, and access documentation.
  • Spectators can attend matches in person but must remain silent. Any disturbance in the competition room will result in expulsion, as per the regulations.

4. Event Schedule

  • 09:00 - Participant reception
  • 09:30 - 10:00 - Tournament and rules presentation
  • 10:00 - 10:30 - Presentation on the history and benefits of chess
  • 10:30 - 11:00 - Break
  • 11:00 - 12:00 - Chess training session
  • 12:00 - 13:00 - Lunch break
  • 13:00 - Competition start
  • 16:00 - End of competition
  • 16:00 - 17:00 - Award ceremony and closing

5. Training Content

  • Introduction to Chess: Origins, history, and impact of the game.
  • Basic Rules: Piece movements, objectives, and victory conditions.
  • Essential Strategies: Center control, piece development, and king safety.

6. Prizes

  • The Group A winner will receive a trophy engraved with their name, which will be displayed at NIT.
  • A chess table and a certificate will be awarded to the winners of Groups A and B.

7. Tournament Rules

  • Tournament Format: Single-elimination bracket.
  • Game Duration: Each game has 10 minutes.
  • Tiebreak Procedure: In case of a draw, a blitz tiebreaker will be played.

Fair Play

  • Cheating is strictly prohibited (e.g., using chess engines or external assistance).
  • Any form of verbal abuse or inappropriate behavior will result in immediate disqualification.

Attendance & Time Limits

  • Players who fail to show up within 10 minutes of their scheduled match will forfeit.

Sanctions for Rule Violations

  • Excessive noise: First warning, then expulsion from the room if repeated.
  • Cheating: Immediate disqualification and ban from future editions.
  • Verbal or physical aggression: Permanent exclusion from the tournament and all future editions.
